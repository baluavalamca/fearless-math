Master-List Coverage Audit

Every one of the 27 sections in foundation_concepts_of_mathematics_master_list.md, checked against the 75 taught lessons and the 17 Advanced tools. Honest verdict: the early foundations are strong; the middle band is partial; the upper band is mostly tools-not-lessons or missing.

One-line answer: No, not everything. The chain number sense → place value → operations → fractions → decimals → percentages → ratio → measurement → basic geometry → data/probability intro → pre-algebra is well taught. The clearest gaps are: order of operations (BODMAS), properties of operations, number-theory depth (primes, HCF/LCM, squares/roots), number systems (irrationals, exponents, scientific notation), geometry relationships (lines/angles types, circles, congruence, Pythagoras, coordinates), full algebra & functions, trigonometry, sets/logic/discrete, and modeling & computational thinking. Several of these exist as Advanced tools but not as taught lessons.
