Life-Skills & Higher-Education Math Review

Not "does it match the NCERT chapter list" — but "does it build the maths a person actually uses in daily life, and the thinking that higher study is built on." Reviewed against the 69 lessons and the Advanced-tools popup. July 2026.

✅ Taught as a lesson 🧰 Exists as an Advanced tool (not a taught lesson) 🟡 Partly covered ❌ Not present
Headline. The everyday "survival maths" is strong: money & change, time & calendar, measuring & unit conversion, estimation & rounding, reading graphs, averages, and mental-maths shortcuts are all taught properly. The gaps are the bridge topics that power both daily decisions and higher study: percentages & discounts, ratio & proportion, probability/chance, speed–distance–time, financial literacy (profit/loss, simple interest, budgeting), integers (negative numbers), and pre-algebra (a letter for an unknown). Several of these exist as 🧰 Advanced calculator tools for Class 6+ but are not yet taught as lessons for the younger learner.

Note on the Advanced tools. Percentage, probability, finance, sets, ratios, graphing and equation-solving already exist as interactive tools in the "Advanced Math (Class 6 → JEE)" popup. That's great for exploring, but a tool lets you compute — it doesn't teach the idea with a story, pictures, worked examples, practice and a mastery check the way the main lessons do. Turning the top items above into taught lessons (with the same fear-free methodology) would close the loop between "can play with it" and "understands and owns it".
