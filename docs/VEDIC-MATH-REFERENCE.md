## History & honest context (read before teaching)

- **Attribution is disputed.** Tirtha claimed the sutras come from an appendix of the Atharvaveda, but historians and mathematicians (e.g. K.S. Shukla) found no such text; the consensus is that this is Tirtha's own **early-20th-century** system, not ancient Vedic knowledge. Teach it as "clever Indian mental-maths shortcuts," not as literal Vedic scripture.
- **Each trick is a special case.** *Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām* (crosswise) is the only truly general multiplication method; the others are fast **only** in their niche (near a base, ending in 5, times 11, etc.). The skill is recognising *when* a shortcut applies.
- **Best used alongside standard methods, not instead of them.** The standard column method builds place-value understanding; the Vedic trick builds speed and number sense. Kids benefit most when they see both and choose.
